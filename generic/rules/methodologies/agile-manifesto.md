# Agile Manifesto

## The Four Values

1. **Individuals and interactions** over processes and tools
2. **Working software** over comprehensive documentation
3. **Customer collaboration** over contract negotiation
4. **Responding to change** over following a plan

Items on the right have value. Items on the left have more value.

## The 12 Principles

1. Satisfy the customer through early and continuous delivery of valuable software
2. Welcome changing requirements, even late in development
3. Deliver working software frequently (weeks, not months)
4. Business people and developers work together daily
5. Build projects around motivated individuals — trust them
6. Face-to-face conversation is the most effective communication
7. Working software is the primary measure of progress
8. Sustainable pace — maintain constant velocity indefinitely
9. Continuous attention to technical excellence enhances agility
10. Simplicity — maximize the work not done
11. Best architectures emerge from self-organizing teams
12. Regularly reflect and adjust behavior

## Agent Behavior

- Principle 10 (simplicity) = don't add features beyond what was asked
- Principle 9 (technical excellence) = ROGYB, SOLID, clean code always
- Principle 12 (reflect and adjust) = retrospective after each goal completion
